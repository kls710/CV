The answer is, mainly yes...

The below verses of the Bible show that almost all sin is equal to God.

James 2:10 - For whosoever shall keep the whole law,
and yet offend in one [point], he is guilty of all.

Matthew 7:1-5 - Judge not, that ye be not judged.

Romans 3:23 - For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God;

James 2:10-11 - For whosoever shall keep the whole law,
and yet offend in one [point], he is guilty of all.

Romans 3:10 - As it is written, There is none righteous, no, not one:

1 John 3:15 - Whosoever hateth his brother is a murderer:
and ye know that no murderer hath eternal life abiding in him.

1 John 5:16-17 - If any man see his brother sin a sin [which is] not unto death,
he shall ask, and he shall give him life for them that sin not unto death.
There is a sin unto death: I do not say that he shall pray for it.

Matthew 12:31 - Wherefore I say unto you, All manner of sin and blasphemy
shall be forgiven unto men:
but the blasphemy [against] the [Holy] Ghost shall not be forgiven unto men.

Galatians 6:7 - Be not deceived; God is not mocked: for whatsoever a man soweth,
that shall he also reap.

Galatians 3:10 - For as many as are of the works of the law are under the curse:
for it is written, Cursed [is] every one that continueth
not in all things which are written in the book of the law to do them.

Mark 7:21-23 - For from within, out of the heart of men,
proceed evil thoughts, adulteries, fornications, murders,

The concept of an unforgivable sin is hard to reconcile with the idea of God
as an energy of infinite love. It does make sense, however, once we realize
that it is not God that forgives. It is we who must forgive ourselves in
order to move forward into the new age to come. We must free ourselves
from the negative consequences of old beliefs, turning instead to the
new beliefs, the new awareness, that comes as we accept ourselves as
the spiritual beings we truly are.

We can't accomplish that forgiveness if we don't believe the Spirit is present
and available. It's not possible to claim a spiritual Truth if we don't
believe its spiritual Source. If we deny our Oneness with the divine,
we are closing ourselves off from the very energy that seeks to embrace
us with infinite love. It's not that God won't forgive us; it's that by
choosing to believe in separation and judgment, we are refusing
to forgive ourselves.

This is why blasphemy against the Holy Ghost is unforgivable.


Examples of forgivable blasphemy.
  • Lying - like Abraham lied about Sarah.
  • Adultery - like when David slept with his very good friends wife.
  • Crime - that Rome deemed worthy of death - The man on the cross next to Jesus.
  • Cursing - Peter

Now there were two men on a cross on either side of Jesus.
And the two are very different from one another. Both commited a crime
punishable of death. But one looked over at Jesus and said,
"If thou beChrist, save thyself and us."
This is blasphemy in the eyes of God. You should never tempt the Lord our God.
Jesus could have legions of angels come to his rescue, he had to finish what
he started.



But the other man rebuked the man saying, "Dost not thou fear God, seeing
thou art in the same condemnation" (sentencing) And we indeed
justly; for we recieve the due reward of our deed:
but this man hath done nothing amiss.
and then he said to Jesus, "Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy
kingdom."

Then that is when Jesus turned to him saying, Verily I say unto thee,
To day shault thou be with me in paradise.

These are from verses 23:39-43 of Luke.


You see, One man did not believe in Jesus, but the other showed that he put
his trust into him.

The difference?

One man is destined for Hell for not believing, for blaspheming His spirit.
The other, he was joined by Jesus in his fathers home in paradise.

Do not doubt that Jesus was who he said he was, God. He was
sent to try and give people a new way to enter the kingdom. If you pass away
from old age or by other means, I pray that you put your faith and trust into
Jesus and believe that he came with the message of salvation, and died for you
on the cross.
