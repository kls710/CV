Does the Bible mention Dinosaurs?
in any of the Books?

In the King James Bible... No. It does not mention the word dinosaurs.
But for a reason...

The word "dinosaur" was not even part of any language
nor thought of until 1842. Long after the King James Bible was translated
from Greek and Hebrew. So it makes perfect sense as to why it is not referenced,
because the word was not invented yet.

The word 'Dinosaur'(translated from: Dinosauria) was invented by a man named Sir Richard Owen.
He was scientist born in 1804, Lancaster, UK. A scientist of biology, anatomist, and paleontology.
Richard is widely known for founding the Natural History Museum in the UK. Also very
widely known for disagreeing with the commonly know 'Darwin' of evolution. Darwin
had Owens helping him on occasions with his work.

So with all that out of the way. Does the Bible 'reference' anything like a dinosaur?
Yes in fact it does.
Back then they refered to them as Dragons. not our modern word 'dinosaur'.
here are a few passages in Scripture that may give a specific reference
to dinosaurs. These passages are found in the Book of Job and they refer to
creatures known as the behemoth and the leviathan.

Behemoth translated from the word behemowth an ancient egyption word,
a diplodocus or brachiosaurus. The exact meaning (which type) is unknown.


leviathan translated from the word livyathan meaning a sea monster,
dragon, extremely large aquatic animal, like the plesiosaurus (which type) is unknown.

Read how these creatures are described in the Bible.

Job 40:15-24
Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox.
Lo now, his strength is in his loins, and his force is in the
navel of his belly.
He moveth his tail like a cedar: the sinews of his stones are
wrapped together.
His bones are as strong pieces of brass; his bones are like
bars of iron.
He is the chief of the ways of God: he that made him can make
his sword to approach unto him.
Surely the mountains bring him forth food, where all the beasts
of the field play.
He lieth under the shady trees, in the covert of the reed, and fens.
The shady trees cover him with their shadow; the willows of
the brook compass him about.
Behold, he drinketh up a river, and hasteth not: he trusteth
that he can draw up Jordan into his mouth.
He taketh it with his eyes: his nose pierceth through snares.
Behemoth described.
  • God made them along side us.
  • They eat grass like an ox, but is not an ox.
  • Moves his tail like cedar, (cedar trees growing between 120 - 180 feet long!).
  • Bones are like beams of bronze.
  • Ribs like bars of iron.
  • They were the first of God.
  • So strong and massive that only God could take it down, (draw near His sword).
  • It's so large that the Nile river gushes into it's mouth.
  • The mountains yeild lots of food, and where it mingles with other creations (animals).

Job 41: 1-2, 7-10
Canst thou draw out leviathan with an hook? or his tongue
with a cord which thou lettest down?
Canst thou put an hook into his nose? or bore his jaw
through with a thorn?
Canst thou fill his skin with barbed irons? or his head
with fish spears?
Lay thine hand upon him, remember the battle, do no more.
Behold, the hope of him is in vain: shall not one be cast
down even at the sight of him?
None is so fierce that dare stir him up: who then is able
to stand before me?
leviathan described
  • This creature is so massive and tough that not even harpoons
    can pierce its skin.
  • Any hope to overcoming it is without any success or results.
  • People would be completely defeated by it (overwhelmed).
  • Not a single person or thing is ferocious or aggressive enough to even
    want to mess with it.
  • Very powerful and libs are of even dimension.
  • There is nothing on land like it's size and strength.

These creatures described are extraordinarily massive!

We have massive animals in modern days like wales and elephants
but the fact earlier men hunted wales and mammoths, doesn't describe the
fact men wouldn't even think twice about messing with one.
